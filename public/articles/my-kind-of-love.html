My kind of love

I cut open my chest and reach into it, rip the heart out, and place it into your soft, elegant hands. Slowly, I close them. My heart is offered to you as a sacrifice. Accept it. For what good is a heart, if it doesn't have a purpose. Take a moment. Look inside of it. And you will know who I truly am. I fall onto the ground, bleeding, gasping for air. As the last dying breath is leaving my body, know that I have always loved you. My kind of love - a poem
